So What Is It?

Conveyor belt sushi is a form of sushi restaurant common in Japan and the United States. The plates with the sushi are placed on a rotating conveyor belt or moat that winds through the restaurant and moves past every table, counter and seat. Customers simply pick their selections from a steady stream of fresh sushi moving along the conveyor belt.

Where Did It Come From?

This style of restaurant dining isn't new. Although different restaurants have adapted this particular way of dining all across the globe, it had its start in Japan.

  2. After five years of development, Shiraishi opened the first conveyor belt sushi Mawaru Genroku Sushi in Higashiosaka in 1958
  3. A conveyor belt sushi boom started in 1970 after a conveyor belt sushi restaurant served sushi at the Osaka World Expo.

Special Orders?

A conveyor belt can sometimes contain more than one hundred different small-portioned sushi, varying from nigiri and sashimi to decadent houserolls. If customers cannot find their desired sushi, the staff can make special orders, and the sushi chef (who is usally stationed inside the conveyor belt), will fill the request order.
